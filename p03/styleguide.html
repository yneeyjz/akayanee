Patchjoi

Patchjoi is a start-up knitted clothing company that has a mission to provide warmth and comfort as customers wear their products.

I started developing the logo with "joi", as part of the company's name, which can be also referred as 'joy'. As Patchjoi hopes their clothing can spark joy and great impression for their customers. This does not only bring the identity but also emphasizes the company's core value.

​Since the company focuses on knitted clothing, the idea of connection and looping came into the inspiration. Based on this concept, I researched and gathered images that inspired me on the general composition.

Inspired by the knitted patterns and the accessories added onto the garments, I developed a composition that is combined with round shapes and lines, yet an invisible grid is hidden behind and hinting the unity among the components. Just like the relationship between products and customers, the company wishes their products are able to create connection with potential customers.

YK PAO

Working with the YK Pao School foundation department, I created the annual sponsorship booklet to introduce the school's education system and to allow the potential audience to grow interests about the sponsorship program. As a relevant marketing source, this project does not simply deliver information to donors and parents of future students but also mantains school's image to the public.

The first step is to gather text content from the foundation department and to group the information into separated pages.

​

To maintain the school's brand identity, we decided to keep the theme color scheme that has been frequently applied to previous marketing materials. The green symbolizes the elements of growth and youth, yet the variety of tones implies the multi-dimensional principle that the school obtains in their education system.

One of the challenges I found was the amount of texts was fixed and was expected to fit into certain pages, In addition, certain images were requested to be added onto specific pages. Hence, I sketched various layouts depends on the length of the content and number of the images would be included on the page. Since the content was broke into different sections, each one of them were designed in specific layout to distinguish from other sections.

For content-heavy sections, the use of images would be minimal, but variation of types applied to enhance hierarchy. (Click images to view detailed layouts)

The following section explains institutional functions available on the campus. Hence, using multiple images would be helpful to allow potential donors understand the information by getting a fuller picture.
